Header image: "Rome," 2018, by Paula Scher

Paula Scher's most notable milestones throughout her life and career:

1948_ Born on October 6 in Washington, DC

1970_ Graduated from Tyler School of Art in Philadelphia, began working at the Random House as a children's book layout artist, and moved to New York City

1972_ Began working at CBS Records in advertising and promotions

1973_ Began working at Atlantic Records as the Cover Art Director and married Seymour Chwast

1974_ Began working at CBS Records again as the Cover Art Director

1978_ Divorced Sermous Chwast

1982_ Became self-employed creating typographic solutions based on Russian Constructivism and Art Deco

1984_ Created Koppel and Scher in partnership with Terry Koppel after briefly working as a freelancer

1989_ Remarried Seymour Chwast

1991_ Coppel and Scher closed, Scher began her partnership with with Pentagram's New York City Office, and she became the Creative Director for Esquire Magazine

1992_ Began working at the School of Visual Arts (SVA) in New York City as a professor while she worked on a variety of other projects

1993_ Became a member of Alliance Graphique Internationale, AGI

1994_ Created the brand identity for the New York's Public Theater

1996_ Won a Beacon Award for integrated corporate identity for the work she did with New York's Public Theater

1998_ Joined the Art Directors' Club of New York Hall of Fame

2000_ Won the Chrysler Award for innovation in design

2001_ Received an AIGA Medal

2002_ Published the book, "Make It Bigger"

2006_ First solo exhibit at the Maya Stendhal Gallery in New York

2007_ Began creating screen prints of New York city, such as "NYC Transit"
